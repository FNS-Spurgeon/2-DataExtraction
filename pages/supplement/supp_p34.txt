Ipomedar.
Small folio, paper, 15th century. The outside leaves of
each quire are vellum. This volume contains minor poems
[p. 189] of Lydgate and Chaucer. Sixteen leaves are wanting,
containing, as appears by the table of contents, the Flower
and the leaf,1 and part of the Complaint of Mars, of which
only the last 32 stanzas are here.
The Declaration of deth of pite. . .
The assemble of ladyes.
The complaint of Annelada against Arcile [sic], the fals
Theban Knight (43 stanzas) ...
The assemble of foules.
The ye and the hert ...
The chorle and the birde—
Envoy ot 8, beginning, "Go little quayer."
Folio, paper, 15th century.—The Temple of Glasse.
