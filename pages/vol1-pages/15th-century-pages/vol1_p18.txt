The Prologe [to King Henry IV].
... þough I vuworþi be I am Maister of this game wiþ
þat noble prince your fadere oure aldere souereyne and liege
lord forsaid. And for I ne wold þat his hunters ne yours þat
now be or shuld come here aftir weren vnknowe in þe profite¬
nesse of þis art for þi shall I leue this symple memorial
ffor as Chaucer saiþ in this prologe of the XXV good wymmen.
Be wryteng haue men of ymages passed for writyng is þe keye
of alle good remembraunce.

My maistre Chaucier / god his soule have /
þat in his langage / was so curyous
He saide þat be fader / nowe dede and grave /
Beqwaþe no-thing / his vertue with his hous /
Vn-to his sone /
(13)
[fol. 26b] By avncetrye þus may yee no-thing clayme
As þat my maistre Chaucier doþe expresse
But temporell thinge / þat man may hurte & mayme
þane is gode stocke / of vertuous noblesse.
..... herke howe my maistre seyde
