1434] Chaucer Criticism and Allusion. 43
As the gold-tressyd / bryght[e] somyr sonne
Passith othir sterrys / with his beemys clere,
And as Lucynya / Chaseth skyës donne,
The frosty nyghtes / whan Esperus doth appere,
Ryght so my1 mayster / had[dë] neuir pere, 1MS. my may
I mene Chaunceer/ in stooryes that he tolde,
And he also wrot / tragedyës Olde.
The Fal of Prynces / gan pitously compleyne
As Petrark did / and also Iohn bochas,
Laureat Fraunceys / Poetys bothe tweyne,
Toold how Prynces / for theer greet trespace,
wer ouirthrowe / Rehersyng al the caas
