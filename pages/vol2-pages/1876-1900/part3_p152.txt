IN HONOREM F. J. F. (A.D. 1900).
(From MS. Harl. 7334, fol. 999, back.)
A Clerk ther was of Cauntebrigge also,
That unto rowing haddè longe y-go.
Of thinnè shidès wolde he shippès makè,
And he was nat right fat, I undertakè.
And whan his ship he wrought had attè fullè,
Right gladly up the river wolde he pullè,
And eek returne as blythly as he wentè.
Him rekkèd nevere that the sonne him brentè,
Ne stinted he his cours for regn ne snowè;
It was a joyè for to seen him rowè!
Yit was him lever, in his shelves newè,
Six oldè textès, clad in greenish hewè,
Of Chaucer and his oldè poesyè
Than ale, or wyn of Lepe, or Malvoisyè.
And therwithal he wex a philosofre;
And peynèd him to gadren gold in cofre
Of sundry folk; and al that he might hentè.
On textes and emprinting he it spentè;
And busily gan bokès to purveyè
For hem that yeve him wherwith to scoleyè.
. . . . . . . . .
Souning in Erly English was his spechè,
"And gladly wolde he lerne, and gladly techè."
