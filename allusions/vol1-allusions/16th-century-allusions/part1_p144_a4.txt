1597. S., J. The Northren Mothers Blessing. The way of Thrift, Written
nine years before the death of G. Chaucer. London, Printed by
Robert Robinson for Robert Dexter, 1597. [in] Certaine Worthye
Manuscript Poems of great Antiquitie Reserued long in the Studie
of a Northfolke Gentleman. And now first published by J. S.
Imprinted at London for R. D. 1597, sign. E 3. (Ed. H. H. Gibbs,
Roxb. club, 1873, bound with The historie of the most noble knight
Plasidas, p. 162 a.)
[There is no allusion whatever to Chaucer in the text. In the B. M. copy, sign.
E 2 b, there is written in a late 17th or early 18th century hand:—"G. Chaucer was
born at Woodstock in Oxfordshire in ye 14th Century, died in 1440." Cf. The Babees
Book, ed. F. J. Furnivall, E. E. T. soc., 1868, Forewords, pp. lxix-lxxi.]
