1598. Peele, George. The Honour of the Garter, Displaied in a Poeme
gratulatorie, to the worthie.... earle of Northumberland, Created
Knight of that Order and install'd at Windsore. Anno Regni
Elizabethæ 35.. Die Junii. 26.... Ad Mœcœnatem Prologus,
sign. A 4 b. (Peele’s Works, ed. A. H. Bullen, 1888, vol. ii, p. 319.)
Why thither [to heauen] post not all good wits from hence,
To Chaucer, Gowre, and to the fayrest Phaer
That euer ventured on great Virgils works?
[There is a good deal of reminiscence of Chaucer's Hous of Fame in the poem itself,
cf. ll. 172-3.]
