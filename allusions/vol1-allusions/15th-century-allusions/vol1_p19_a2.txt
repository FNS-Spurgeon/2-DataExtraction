¶ A commendacioun of Chaucers. [Harl 629. fol. 43 b-44]
nd eke my master Chauceris nowe is graue
A
The noble rethor Poete of breteine
That worthy was the laurer to haue
Of peetrie [sic] and the palme atteine
That made firste to distille and reyne
The golde dewe droppis of speche and eloquence
In-to oure tounge thourȝ his excellence
¶ And founde the flourys first of rethoryk
Oure rude speche oonly to enlumyne
That in oure tunge was neuer noon him like
For as the sunne dothe in heuen shyne
In mydday speere dovn to vs by-lyne [fol. 44]
In whos presence noo sterre may appere
Riȝt so his ditees withoute any peere
¶ Eny makyng with his liȝt disteine
In sothfastnesse who so taketh heede
Wherfor noo wondre thouȝ myn herte pleyne
Vpon his dethe and for sorowe blede
For wante of him nowe in my greet[e] nede
That shulde allas conveie and directe
And with his supporte amende and corecte
¶ The wronge tracys of my rude penne
Ther as y erre and goo nouȝt Iyne riȝt
But for that he ne may me not kenne
I can no more but with alle my myȝt
With alle myne herte and myne inward siȝt
Praye for him that nowe lieth in cheste
To god above to ȝiue his soule good reste
¶ And as y can forthe y wil procede
Sithen of his helpe ther may noo socour bee
And thourȝ my penne ay quakyng for drede
Neither to. Cloie. ne to. Caliope
Me liste not calle for to helpe me
Ne to no muse my pointel for to guye
But leue alle this and seie to Marie
O clene castel and the chaste toure
Of the hooly goost modir and virgine
Be thou my helpe &c. ...
