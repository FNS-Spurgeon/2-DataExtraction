[c. 1400.] Lydgate, John. The Chorle and the bird. Last stanza.
MS. Harb 116, fol. 152. (Lydgate’s minor poems, ed. J. O. Halli¬
well, 1840, Percy soc., vol. ii, p. 193.)
Go gentill quayer, and Recommaunde me
Vnto my maistir with humble affectioun
Beseke hym lowly of mercy and pite
Of thy rude makyng to haue compassioun
And as touching thy translacioun
Oute of frensh / hough euer the englisshe be
Al thing is saide undir correctioun
With supportacioun of your benignite.
