1869. Galton, Francis. Hereditary Genius: an Inquiry into its Laws
and Consequences. London, 1869, pp. 226, 228, 230.
[Galton examines the relationships of 56 poets, 20 of these
among whom is Chaucer, have had, he says, eminent kinsfolk;
the following are his facts about Chaucer.]
[p. 230] Chaucer, Geoffrey; wrote the "Court of Love" æt. 18.
Illustrious poet; father of English poetry and, in some sense,
of the English language also.
S[on], Sir Thomas; was Speaker of the House of Commons
and ambassador to France.
