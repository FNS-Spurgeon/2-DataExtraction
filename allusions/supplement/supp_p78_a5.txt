1880. [Furnivall, F. J.] Note under Notes and News, [in] The Academy,
London, 26th June, vol. xvii, p. 472.
[Referring to a quarto volume of MS. acquired by Prof.
Dowden, lettered "Chaucer's Canterbury Tales Modernised,
Vol. 2." Neither the writer’s name nor that of any former
owner appears in the volume. The water-mark of the paper
is 1811. An extract given from the "Prioresse Tale" is
given. Query: Where is the first volume of this nineteenth
century Chaucer to be found ?]
