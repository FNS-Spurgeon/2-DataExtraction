[p. 11] Hasty generalisers are apt to overlook the fact, that the
Saxon was never, to any great extent, a literary language.
Accordingly, it held its own very well in the names of common
things, but failed to answer the demands of complex ideas,
derived from them. The author of "Piers Ploughman" wrote
for the people,—Chaucer for the court.
