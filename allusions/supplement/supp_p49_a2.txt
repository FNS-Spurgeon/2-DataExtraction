1874. Furnivall. F. J. Discussion on F. G. Fleay's paper, On Certain
Plays of Shakspere, read before New Shakspere Society, June 12,
1874, printed after the text of the paper in Transactions of
New Shakspere Society, 1874, p. 328.
Shakespeare’s Re-use of Former Sources. . . .
1. Chaucer. Shakspere used his Knight's Tale in the
early Midsummer-Night's Dream and the late Two Noble
Kinsmen. Chaucer's Troilus, Shakspere used in early
life, in Troilus and The Two Gentlemen. Gamelyn—then
supposed to be Chaucer’s—Shakspere may have used in
As You Like It of his middle time.
