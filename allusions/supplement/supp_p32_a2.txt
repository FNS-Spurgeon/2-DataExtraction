1871. Ruskin, John. Fors Clavigera, vol. I., Letter 5 (May 1871).
Works of Ruskin, ed. E. T. Cook and A. Wedderburn, 1907,
vol. xxvii, p. 84.
There are no such things as Flowers—there are only—
gladdened Leaves. Nay, farther than this, there may be a
dignity in the less happy, but unwithering leaf, which is,
in some sort, better than the brief lily of its bloom;—which
the Greek poets always knew,—well;—Chaucer, before
Goethe; and the writer of the first Psalm before Chaucer.
