1871. Furnivall, F. J. Jyl of Breyntford's Testament ... [and] A
Balade or two by Chaucer, ed. F. J. Furnivall, London. Printed
for Private Circulation, 1871, pp. 5, 6, 25n., 34-6, 38.
[The first balade, apparently ascribed to Chaucer by Shirley,
is the one of which Dr. Furnivall had previously in 1871
printed two stanzas: see above. It comes from Shirley's MS.,
Addit. 16, 165, and begins:
'Hit is no right alle oþar lustes to leese,' etc.
The second, from the same MS., begins:
'Of alle þe crafftes oute / blessed be þe ploughe,' etc.
The third piece is the 'Proverbe of Chaucer,' beginning
What shal þees cloþes þus many fold] (Skeat, Minor Poems,
XX, p. 407).
