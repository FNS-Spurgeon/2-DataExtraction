1869. Wright, Thomas. Churchwarden’s Account of the Town of

[Note upon an entry in the churchwarden's account "for
mendynge and fasteninge the window over the wedinge
churche dore," calling attention to the frequent mention of
the wedding door in the accounts, and to the fact, that before
the Reformation important parts of the wedding and baptism
services, etc., were performed in the church porch.] The
reader of Chaucer will remember what he says (Cant. T.,
l. 462) of the Wife of Bath,—
"Housbandes atte chirche dore hadde sche fyfe."
