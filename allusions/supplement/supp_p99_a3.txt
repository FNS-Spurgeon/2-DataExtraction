1884. Sanborn, Kate. One view of the Chaucerian Mania [in] The
Manhattan, March-June, p. 367.
[p. 369] It would be idiotic to underrate Chaucer. He will ever be
invaluable to the historian, antiquarian and philologist. My
plea is for the young students who need to know so much
besides.
[A long and violent attack on the study of Chaucer by the
general reader.]
... What the young folk want of Chaucer is his descrip¬
tions of English life and character, his humour, pathos, and
satire, and they can be given them in a dozen pages.
