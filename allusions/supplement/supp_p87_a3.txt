1881. Kelly, E. R. (editor) Kelly's Directory of Nottinghamshire,
Leicestershire and Rutland and Derbyshire, fifth edition, London,
1881. Under Leicestershire, p. 504, col. i.
Castle Donington.
[p. 503,
There was anciently a castle on the north-east side of the
col. 2]
town, of which some remains still exist . . . it was built
[p. 504] by Henry Laci, Earl of Lincoln (who died in 1310): Clark,
in his "Riches of Chaucer," says it was once the residence
of that poet, being presented to him for his life by his brother-
in-law "John of Gaunt." . . . The deer park embraces
400 acres of romantic scenery; the oak trees are a great
attraction, one "Chaucer's Oak" being 44 ft. girth, at a
height of 12 ft. from the ground.
[These remarks about Chaucer first occur in this edition,
not in any earlier one (1848 ff.). They are identical in the
sixth edition of 1888. In the seventh edition, 1891, the
remark from Cowden Clark is omitted, and that about
'Chaucer’s Oak' only retained, which latter remark is to be
found also in the current edition of 1912, p. 55, col. 2.]
