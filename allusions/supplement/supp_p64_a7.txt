1878. Furnivall, F. J. Note to the "Balade" of Chaucer to his Empty
Purse (see Nicholas's Life of Chaucer, ed. Morris, vol. i, p. 40) [in]
Temple Bar, Sept. 1878, vol. liv, p. 144.
The above mentioned "Balade" was sent to Henry IV, on
his accession, with the following Envoye, which I have slightly
modernized:—
"O Conqueror of Brut-es Albion,
Which that by line and free election
Be very King, this song to you I send;
And thou that mayest all mine harms amend,
Have mind upon my supplication."
And, doubtless in answer to this appeal, Henry IV doubled
Chaucer's pension within four days after he came to the
throne, by granting him on Oct. 3rd, 1399, forty marks
yearly, in addition to the annuity which King Edward had
given him.
