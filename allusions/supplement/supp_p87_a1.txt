1881. Grant, Charles. The Fairy Land of Shakespeare [in] Gentleman's
Magazine, London, April, 1881, vol. ccl, p. 434.
Both in the Decameron and The Canterbury Tales we
find the same delight in the facts of life, the same clear eye
for realistic detail, which distinguished the English drama
of the Elizabethan age. ... But the interest which was
awakened by the manners and actions of men was afterwards
concentrated on their characters and passions. We find in
Boccaccio, and still more frequently in Chaucer, many traces
of a personal leaning towards the later manner. The descrip¬
tion of the pilgrims, the quarrel between the Reeve and the
Miller, the Wife of Bath’s Prologue, and, indeed, most of
the incidents by the way, are conceived in the spirit of the
Elizabethan dramatists.
