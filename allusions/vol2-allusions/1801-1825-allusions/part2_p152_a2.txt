1824. S., W. Letter [in] The Gentleman's Magazine, May, vol. xciv,
pp. 411-12.
Whan hys lotte was to wake a night
[to]
... horne pipis of Cornewayle.
[Rom. Rose, ll. 4248-50.]
[Chaucer probably mistook Cornouaille, the Corneil or wild
cherry tree, for Cornewaille–Cornwall. This occurs in the
non-Chaucerian portion of the Romance of the Rose. See
Skeat’s note on the line.]
