1818. Keats, John. Robin Hood; to a Friend. (Complete Works of
John Keats, ed. H. Buxton Forman, 5 vols., 1900-01, vol. ii,
p. 118, vol. iv, p. 73 [in a letter to J. H. Reynolds of 3 Feb. 1818].)
Gone, the merry morris din;
Gone the song of Gamelyn;
Gone, the tough-belted outlaw
Idling in the “grenè shawe."
[Cf. "Where ridestow under this grene shawe," Freres Tale, line 88. Similarly Keats
uses the phrase "Ah! hertè mine" in a letter (11 Oct. 1819, Works, vol. v, p. 129).
This occurs passim in Troilus, but is common in old poetry.]
